About Falāh

Falāh: success in this world and the next.

IIB Falāh is the dedicated Shariah window of the Investcent Investment Bank Unit Trust Fund, built so that faith and finance never have to be traded against each other.

Who We Are

The Shariah window of Investcent

IIB Falāh combines Investcent's active investment discipline with rigorous, independent Shariah governance. Every product, contract and counterparty is certified by our Shariah Supervisory Board before launch and monitored continuously thereafter.

Governance

Shariah Supervisory Board

A qualified, independent SSB reviews, approves and monitors every investment, certifies every transaction structure, and reviews the entire portfolio quarterly. Brochures and documents are reviewed for Shariah accuracy before distribution.

Screening

What We Will Never Invest In

No conventional interest-based banking or insurance, alcohol, pork-related products, gambling, adult entertainment, tobacco or non-defensive weapons. Financial thresholds: interest-bearing debt and interest-bearing cash each below 33% of assets; non-compliant revenue below 5%.

Ethics

Maqasid al-Shariah Scoring

Holdings must achieve a minimum impact score of 60/100 across five pillars: Faith, Life, Intellect, Lineage and Wealth. The Preservation of Life pillar specifically scores environmental sustainability and workplace safety.

Purification and reserves, explained.

Any inadvertent non-permissible income is identified, isolated and directed to charity before distribution. To smooth returns across strong and weak periods, up to 2% of the net profit pool may be appropriated into a Profit Equalisation Reserve, and a further portion may be retained into an Investment Risk Reserve. The two reserves are jointly capped at 30% of NAV, and the full mechanism is set out in Section 21 of the Shariah Information Memorandum.

Profit Equalisation Reserve (PER)Applied proportionately before distribution, so the cost is shared by all with an interest in that period's profit.
Investment Risk Reserve (IRR)Retained after your share is calculated; returned pro-rata if the Fund is ever wound up.
Charity purificationNon-permissible income never reaches your returns.

How It Works

The contracts behind your investment.

Islamic finance replaces interest-based lending with genuine trade, agency and lease contracts. These are the structures IIB Falāh uses.

Wakalah

Agency for Investment

You appoint Investcent as your Wakil (investment agent) to manage your capital for a defined Wakalah Fee. Used across our fund products; the money market and fixed account products use Wakalah only, with no performance share.

Mudarabah

Profit Sharing

On growth products, Investcent acts as Mudarib and shares only in profit above a demanding hurdle rate. Capital losses are borne by you as Rab al-Maal, except where caused by proven negligence, misconduct or breach of the Shariah mandate.

Murabaha & Salam

Asset-Backed Trade

Cost-plus and forward-sale contracts over real, permissible commodities. Every transaction is backed by an actual underlying asset and a genuine sale contract; no speculative futures, CFDs or derivatives.

Ijarah

Lease

The Fund acts as lessor over real property and leased assets, earning rental income (Ujrah). As owner, the Fund bears major maintenance and Takaful costs, consistent with genuine Ijarah. Rentals are fixed or determinable, never floating against an interest benchmark.

Wa'd

Unilateral Promise

A binding one-sided promise used for Shariah-compliant hedging of a genuine underlying exposure. Only the promisor is bound; no exchange contract exists until the promise is invoked and a real sale is executed.

Sukuk

Islamic Certificates

Asset-based certificates used across our money market, green and multi-asset products, including green Sukuk whose proceeds are earmarked for climate and environmental projects.

Fees that only win when you win.

Growth products carry a Wakalah Fee of 5% p.a. prorated over 365 days, plus a Performance Charge only above the hurdle: 10% of returns above a 25% hurdle on KES classes, or 15% of returns above a 15% hurdle on USD classes. Conservative products (Money Market and Fixed Account) charge a flat 1.5% p.a. Wakalah Fee with no performance share at all. Ijarah products charge an estimated 1.5% p.a. asset management fee with an income incentive above a 15% yield hurdle, pending final SSB confirmation.

FAQs

Questions investors ask us.

How IIB Falāh keeps your investments halal, and the practicalities of investing.

How do I open an IIB Falāh account?
Complete the IIB Falāh Account Opening & KYC Form, then the Investment Application Form, selecting your chosen product in the Fund Selection Schedule. Transfer your investment to the Fund's dedicated Shariah bank account, and units are allocated at the next Valuation Point after cleared funds are received.
Who certifies that the products are Shariah-compliant?
An independent, qualified Shariah Supervisory Board (SSB) reviews, approves and monitors every investment, certifies every contract structure before use, and re-screens all holdings quarterly. Product brochures are reviewed by the SSB before distribution.
What happens if a holding becomes non-compliant?
It is reclassified and divested under SSB oversight: immediately if in profit, or conditionally held to breakeven if at a loss. Any non-permissible income identified is purified by donation to charity before distribution.
What fees will I pay?
Conservative products (Money Market Shariah Fund and Shariah Fixed Account Fund) charge a flat 1.5% p.a. Wakalah Fee with no performance share. Growth products charge a 5% p.a. Wakalah Fee plus a Performance Charge only above the hurdle: 10% above a 25% hurdle (KES) or 15% above a 15% hurdle (USD). There are no initial or redemption fees on fund products.
Who bears investment losses?
In line with Mudarabah principles, capital losses are borne by you as the capital provider (Rab al-Maal), except where a loss results from Investcent's proven negligence, wilful misconduct or breach of the Fund's Shariah mandate, in which case Investcent bears the loss.
What are the PER and IRR reserves?
The Profit Equalisation Reserve (up to 2% of the net profit pool, applied before distribution) smooths returns across periods, and the Investment Risk Reserve (retained after your share is calculated) cushions future losses and is returned pro-rata on wind-up. They are jointly capped at 30% of NAV; see Section 21 of the Shariah Information Memorandum.
How liquid are the products?
Money Market and Fixed Account products have a two-week lock-in with redemptions typically processed within 2 working days. Growth products have a 6-month lock-in with redemptions in 2 to 3 working days, and no exit fee. Ijarah products (Lease ETF and REIT) require a minimum 30-day redemption notice.
Can institutions and chamas invest?
Yes. Retail, institutional and group investors can invest, subject to minimum investment amounts and KYC requirements.
Who regulates IIB Falāh?
All products operate under the regulatory framework of the Capital Markets Authority, Kenya, alongside continuous Shariah oversight by the SSB.
